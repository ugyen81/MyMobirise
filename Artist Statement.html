Artist Statement

My passion for art picked up its momentum originally from Sherubtse Public School (former name for Sherubtse College) where the primary students from Class 1 to 5 were given one art class session once a week. Our art classes constituted replication of sample images (a dragon and eight lucky signs) drawn on blackboard by art teacher who would correct our artwork. 
My passion for art further deepened from successful achievement in Class X, (ICSE) Art Exam in 1980. The art exam consisted of pencil drawing and sketching of basket-full still images of fruits and plants. Since then drawing and painting pictures has been at the core of my interest.
The medium of my art work is mainly oil on canvas. The first oil portrait that I painted was our Royal Couple which was gifted to Their Majesties on their grand wedding ceremony on 14th Oct, 2011.
The painting process starts from basic sketching and drawing of subject with help of Grid Drawing Tool which is freely available online in http://www.griddrawingtool.com. I always use this grid drawing tool rather than directly copying and drawing the reference images in order to retain the same design, format and shape of the subject especially when enlarging them on canvas.  
After image reference drawing is complete, I first block the major shapes, designs and forms of the subject with acrylic as a first step to laying the foundation of artwork. However all the major work of painting is done in oil. I prefer oil over acrylics as it gives me sufficient opportunity to blend the colors and adjust the value and tonality of the painting with its slow drying properties.  
My artwork mainly consists of Landscapes, Portraits, Wild Life and other Figurative paintings. 
Every new painting is a new experience of learning for me. I feel I've been blessed with a special gift and it's incredibly satisfying to know that I can bring joy into the lives of others through my paintings.
